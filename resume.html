University of Chicago Laboratory Schools

I've gone to the University of Chicago Laboratory Schools since I was three, so in essence I spent my entire childhood at Lab School. Lab School is ranked one of the top private schools in the country. I'm expected to graduate this year in early June. As of the spring quarter, I'll be graduating with a 3.86 unweighted GPA.

EdX - Future Cities

This was an onlince class I took in conjunction with an architecture independent study. This class focused on the layouts and process of building modern cities. I recieved an A in this class.

EdX - Big Bang and the Origin of Chemical Elements

This online course was taken again in conjunction with another class: Cosmology. Cosmology is a winter physics elective offered at Lab School. I took the spring physics elective as well: Modern Physics. I recieved an A in this class.

Chicago Architecture Foundation - Architecture Teen Studios

This studio class was taken to supplement my architecture independent study. It was 10 weeks of in-class work, where we worked with professional architects and architecture software. From this class, we entered and won in multiple categories.

Experience Ignite - Strategy & Marketing Associate

Researched and developed PR and brand strategy, helped develop drip campaign, developed social media posts, created ads/promotional content. I interned for the summer of my rising senior year starting in June. Experience Ignite is a startup specializing in the betterment of workplace management through positive, global experiences.

Hyde Park Youth Symphony - Volunteer Asst General Manager

I volunteered at my neighborhood's Youth Symphony orchestra where I was the assistant to the general manager. Additionally, I worked under the head of the organization helping designing public relation visuals (a new logo, posters, mailing cards, and invites).

Spoon University - High School Reporter

Spoon University is an online food publication entirely produced by college and high school students. Content includes recipes, life advice, health advice, city favorites, and much more. I started writing for Spoon High School the summer of my rising junior year. I've written about cricket flour and non-animal meat to my favorite korean dishes. Check out my articles here.

UChicago Press Summer Internship

I interned at the University of Chicago's Press summer of my rising junior year. I served under Christie Henry, who, at the time, was the director of the UChicago Press. My responsibilities included: proofreading undergraduate textbooks, editing a college essay writing guide by Rachel Toor, organizing inventory, and excerpting and compiling book reviews for the marketing department.

Landscaping, Dog Walking, Tutoring

Landscaping: My mother created her own local Hyde Park landscaping company a few years back, maintaining landscapes for several condominiums and houses. I've been mostly involved in the manual labor aspect of the enterprise. I work for her during the summer and early fall seasons.

Dog Walking: I locally walk dogs for neighbors and other members of Hyde Park. I have my own dog, but I rarely walk my dog with other dogs because my dog isn't a huge fan of other dogs. Please contact me if you need a dog walker!

Tutoring I tutored an 8th grader last year in French, math, and humanities for my spring quarter. I was offered the position again to tutor chemistry to the same kid, however, due to time complications I had to deny. I did find a good replacement. Contact me if you're interested in tutoring!

Asian Students Association - Treasurer

I've been a part of Lab School's Asian Students Association (ASA) since freshman year. I joined the board junior year as treasurer and remained treasurer through senior year. Contributions include: coordinating UChicago guest speaker, Mina Lee, presentation; Advocating for educational/cultural projects.

Renaissance - President

Renaissance is Lab School's art and literature magazine. I co-direct the art side of this enterprise. As of this year, we've made a point of expanding our mediums into performance arts as well as the usual visual pieces. I've initiated two major changes: the implementation of our website and the involvement of Renaissance with Labstock and Labarts.

Student Council - All-School Vice President

I've been on student council for all four years of high school. My first three years were as grade vice president and my final year was as indicated–all-school vice president. I've found my job to be more a voice for the unpopular opinions than. Student council is responsible for being a vehicle between the student body and the rest of the parts of the school, mainly the administrative body. You can find out more here.

Finance Club - Club Member

I joined Finance Club in my sophomore year. Our activities included creating an investment porfolio, touring investment firms, and bringing in speakers of various sorts relating to finance. My best experience during my time with the Finance Club was our visit to Goldman Sachs.

Varsity Soccer – 4 years

I've played Lab School Varsity soccer since my freshman year. Last year, under new coaching staff, we proceeded to super sectional finals–the two steps away from the state championship. This made Lab School history by breaking the 8-year drought.

Yearbook Journalism - Editor in Chief

I've been a member of Lab's yearbook staff for the entirety of high school. From the title of reporter, I became sports editor, life editor, then finally Editor in Chief. Yearbook has been a large part of my high school career. I've spent many a late-night and Saturday working on yearbook layouts. In addition to the opportunity to learn about journalism, I've been introduced to design.

Maroon Key Society - Student Ambassador

As a result of being elected to student council, I was offered the opportunity to join the Maroon Key Society–a group of student ambassadors for Lab's high school. We help retell our experiences at Lab to potential students, families, and sometimes alumni. Often our duties include Q&A panels or tours of the historic campus.

Classical Guitar

I've played classical guitar for six years. I stopped receiving lessons two years back, so now I mostly play on my own for my own. I never did any major performances, instead I played simply for the sake of wanting to learn guitar. I learned classical guitar just by chance.

Club Soccer – SSF, Chicago City

I've played club soccer the majority of my life. Starting with AYSO, I went on to play for South Side Fire FC for something like seven or so years. I served as captain for most of my time on South Side, and played goalkeeper and midfield (although I can and have played quite literally every postion on the field). My last year of club soccer was with Chicago City FC.
