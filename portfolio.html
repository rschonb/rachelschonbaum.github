Artist’s Statement

Although I don’t quite have one unifying theme or method for my artwork, the process is often the same with my mixed media pieces. The sketchbook, tire vest and cover are all examples of work where I’ll have an idea of what I want the end-product to look like, and I’ll just try out different ways and mediums to get to that image in my mind. I’m fond of using some blend of calculations and . I’m inspired by a vast number of traditional and modern artists, from Chicago-favorite Hebru Brantley to France’s Gustave Guillaumet. In my photography, people portraits that show show some sort of uniqueness or some raw relationship are the best pieces.

I'd like someday to work on more traditional art. There are a few occasions where I've been content with my paintings. Maybe I'll do some surreal work or graphics too. Who knows. Stay tuned for more.
